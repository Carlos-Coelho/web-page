Carlos M. Agra Coelho
 

 

e-mail: cmac@fct.unl.pt

 

 

 

Ph.D. in Biostatistics by the  University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, MI, U.S.A.

     Full Professor at the Mathematics Department of the NOVA School of Science and Technology of  NOVA University of Lisbon. Lectures courses in Statistics at the undergraduate and graduate levels.

President of the Portuguese Fulbright Alumni Association. 

Research interests: Multivariate Analysis (AMS 62H), namely Likelihood Ratio Tests in Multivariate Analysis; Mathematical Statistics and distribution theory (AMS 62E). Exact, near-exact  and asymptotic distributions of statistics, namely LRT statistics used in Multivariate Analysis. Statistics and inferential processes related with Canonical Analysis and Generalized Canonical Analysis (in particular, the Wilks' Lambda statistic), their exact, near-exact and asymptotic distributions, characteristic and moment generating functions. Exact, near-exact and asymptotic distributions of sums of independent Gamma with different scale parameters and products of independent Beta random variables.

            More recently also the topic of Statistical Disclosure Control (SDC) and LRTs (likelihood ratio tests) for the high-dimensional case are topics of interest.
